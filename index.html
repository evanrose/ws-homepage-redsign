Case Study

HEMNET: THE HOUSE OF CLICKS

We set out to show that Hemnet is not only a pioneer of property portals — but also the future of property development. And we did so by turning ones and zeros into bricks and mortar. Our team of data scientists analyzed more than 200 million clicks, unearthing the foundation of what Swedes dream about in a future home.

Based on the data, Hemnet built an actual House of Clicks — a gorgeous 1.5 story home featuring a red wooden facade and a private rooftop terrace.

Co-created with more than 2 million Swedes, this campaign turned big data into a truly big idea.

We also created a whole new market, based on data only we possess.

So far, more than 648 people have signed up to buy the house. Perhaps Contagious, in its annual Cannes contenders article, put it best. “We’ve all seen examples of data viz in the past, but visualizing data as an actual house — well, that sounds pretty creative to me.”

Case Study

DAUGHTERS OF MOTHER INDIA

IGNITING A MOVEMENT OF CHANGE AND HOPE

Filmmaker Vibha Bakshi asked Weber Shandwick to help launch her documentary, the story of a brutal Delhi gang rape and its aftermath. But instead of simply introducing this film to consumers, we first engaged the most important stakeholder — and an unexpected one — the police. The nation’s police force is comprised of individuals from within this society where women are often not respected, so we sought to gender sensitize its officers. We also leveraged compelling movie theater PSAs, a first-of-its-kind television simulcast in 8 languages and media outreach to engage millions of citizens.

The campaign is making a real difference already. To date, 150,000 police officers have been trained. The film is now a mandatory part the National Police Academy curriculum. Rare – and critically important – partnerships between local elders and police have been formed. And most importantly, early estimates already show a 20% increase in reported sex crimes.

Case Study

MATTEL BARBIE: EVOLVE THE DOLL

For more than 57 years, Barbie has been a global, cultural icon and a source of inspiration and imagination to millions of girls around the world. But as with any legacy brand, evolution was necessary to stay relevant for the next generation.

To better reflect what girls see in the world, Barbie announced the expansion of its Fashionistas® line with the addition of three new body types – tall, curvy and petite – and a variety of skin tones, hair styles and outfits. With these choices, girls everywhere will have infinitely more ways to play out their stories and spark their imaginations through Barbie.

Barbie is a brand people love to hate. Launching new body types is one thing, to have the world recognize Barbie as a beacon for change is another.

But by building on Barbie’s history of inspiring girls to pursue careers in anything from space travel to politics, and involving experts, opinion leaders and key media, Barbie went from an unlikely brand voice for girls’ empowerment to the natural opinion leader and game changer. We took the global conversation from a place of controversy back to where it belongs. Focused on how the world’s most famous doll empowers young girls through play.
